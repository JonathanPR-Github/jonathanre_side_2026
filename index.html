"THE FIRST TRIES"

2016 - 2018

The First Tries, we're the first batch of maps i created and released around 2018, at that time i was 15 years old.

Beginning around 2017 and ending late in 2018 during a time when i made these in my free time after school, i created 5 quite simple maps in the world builder for the Source Engine called "Hammer", that initially begun as curiouseness and shortly fell into being fun. This was about the first time when i realized how fun i found development to be, as throughout the time i made them, i found making them more enjoyable for whatever i was doing during that time at school

Unfortently however it would take me quite some time to realize that i should have put most of my work into building a career, so for must of that time i was only curious

Prior to creating these 5 maps, at which a time i saw nothing too the engine, i was making small levels with friends in Roblox based around things i played on that platform, but these we're merely early attempts

"THE DEATHRUN SAGA COLLECTION"

2018 - 2025

The Deathrun Saga Collection was a collection of 3 Deathrun maps for "Team Fortress 2" developed within the span of 2024 and 2025

Deathrun is a community created game mode in which where one player controls traps in an obstacle course while the rest of the players attempt to survive and reach the end of the map. It emphasizes timing, player interaction, and asymmetrical gameplay design, with adventure designing heavily emphasised.

Starting right after i finished my first attempts at making maps in 2018, i felt at the time like i wanted to try to create something that wasn't going to try to follow the offical modes, but that i wanted to expand to the custom modes made by the playerbase. It would still take me some time to go back to the Hammer as i was going back and forth with school, and with that i paused the map for a whole two years before contiuning on it

But around 2020 i finally came around to finish what would become my first proper map with its alpha. After finishing it i contiuned to develop on it over the next 3 years, to note this was also around a time where i was simultaneously learning the engine, so it would natrually also take a bit of time for me to work things around

During the maps development, i learnt the value of teamwork even as i was making it myself, when i was helped by various people internationally that was simiarly also making Deathrun maps, in which i could also lend help by teaching people things they didn't know. This was also when i learnt the value of playtesting and taking feedback, as whe playtested my map everytime it had a new addition to it, i would rescive valuable negative and positive feedback that would help me shape how i could improve my projects for the best

Due to something i can only describe as a Dilemma, i had a constant worry that i had to perfect the map as much as possible, which is also why it took so long to develop. This is where i also developed a thought process a project should always be developed within a certain timeframe and never to be developed for unnecessary amounts of lenghts of time. In the end i had to set my foot down, and decide that the map had to be released, in which in 2024 Dustgrowl was finally released. From there map development would go much faster, as the next dr maps i would release would only take a few weeks

DUSTGROWL

2024

Dustgrowl was designed very special, as all of the other deathrun maps i would create, they would have something unique that would make them stand out, and that included the first proper map i made

It is made unique by that there is two whole course levels in the map, meaning that after playing 3 rounds, or winning the game it switches over the map to an entierely new course with completely different traps to change up the gameplay, and so back to the first course by the same gameplay loop. This map made it stand out between other deathrun maps, besides its obvious higher quality in vision and grapichs from other deathrun maps at the time

Dustgrowl released to quite favorable reviews from the playtesters who had played it, as most of the maps playerbase came from those who tested the map. It was praised for both it looks and gameplay mechanics

TIMBERCROVE

2024

Timercrove was designed to try to see if an open world for a deathrun map would work, with different builds building upon that idea gradually as i was making the map over the span of 2 months

It is made unique by that the players spawn around a hut which is the main objective around a arena of obstacle courses that are explorable by completing each one of them seperatly, and then to beat each course, you must complete a puzzle by each end of the areas

Timbercrove released to favorable reviews from the playtesters who had played it. However it was never truly added to any of the remaining servers at the time hosting the gamemode, so it was never truly praised

SPIRALSTOVE

2025

Spiralstove was designed around a "pick your adventure" type of gameplay, with the map building upon allowing players to choose their own deathrun course, with the making of the map over the span of 2 months

It is made unique by that a singular player at the start of the match gets to pick out a course of 3 portals infront of them, that eventually leads them to playing a course with completely unique themes to them

Spiralstove released to favorable reviews from the playtesters who had played it and both the players playing it.

Before i left the Deathrun community with this map, i had 62 mappers and testers sign the map in a wavour to send me off to the game dev world, a truly connecting experience between a community

"DOVREGUBBEN"

2023 - 2024

Dovregubben codenamed "Game6" was a short sidescroller video game developed in Godot to promote a product for the company i was guidance assigned to called "Funkweb"

It was developed by mostly everyone that we're assigned to that company to guidance, which we're mostly made up of webdesigners. It total some 20 people worked on it on and off, as the project was something that could just be worked on and off on.

During the development of the game, i was the level designer and partial manager, where i developed the only level that would be end up being used and suggested the framework for how the game should play. The level i designed was made in a level designer tool that i found when starting my work with Godot to reduce the workflow

Dovregubben story revolved around the character "Dovregubben" who loves going skiing, one day in his cabin a giant troll steals his skiis and runs away with all of them, Dovregubben sees this and makes chase after him on one of the skiis the troll left behind

The game was planned to have 10 levels, boss battles, a store between each of the second levels, a currency system that could be used to buy ugrades for the characther and customizable outfits. However due to time constraints and lack of motivation from the team, only 1 level was made which would consist of the whole game

Dovregubben released early 2024 with favorable reviews, though becouse the game was unfinished it never took properly off

"RED DUSK"

2024 - 2025

Red Dusk is a total overhaul mod for the game Heart's of Iron 4, set in a alternative reality world where the August Coup in the Soviet Union succeeded

The game starts on the first of January 2000, and becouse of the existence of the Soviet Union, some dictorships in the world that would have gone away by that time still exists

I started on Red Dusk as a coder, by implemnting Red Dusk their new color scheme, sitting and painstakingly setting each countries color, then once i had completed that, i made the mods map font and map style that would over time become iconic. After having done that i started to implement a lot of guidelines for the mod on how flavoured it should be, by fixiating at adding small details to the mod, updating leaders to be historically accurate and adding more locilization flavour

When developing on Red Dusk, the team and i extensively used Github with pull requests and branches to cordinate between ourself to elevate the workflow between the team's members

About a month into the mod i was also elevated to a "Directing Coder", which is essentially a coder with more elevated freedom to do things their way

At the time around when i joined, they we're working on a update that would eventually become the "Across the Atlantic Update", where i coded a few political branches of the UK, a cut path for the United States, Civil War events in India and Pakistan, Civil war intervention mechanics and much much more

"ACROSS THE ATLANTIC - UPDATE"

2025

Across the Atlantic released with quite favourable reviews by both the players who played it and youtubers who commercialized it

The update released with the new map style, the focus tree for the United Kingdom and more improvements to the mods storyline

Due to the low development team we had, we unfortently had to cut most of the content that was originally planned for the update

After Across the Atlantic, we begun working on the next update to the mod, where i worked on Iran's content paths, China's content paths and some new game mechanics.

Iran was going to have a few paths, where it would either devolve into a civil war or contiune down its current path. One for the current goverment, one for a Communist regime and one for either a Shaist or Democratic path. I worked on both extensively for a couple of months with another developer, while doing other tasks on the mod.
China was going to have something similar, with it mostly revolving around the CCP, but that it could also fall into civil war if it lost a war with Taiwan. I only coded the Warlords that would come from the Chinese Civil War.

Around that time i begun complaining about the mods lack of development phase, which lead me to start to create a country path for Norway.

The game for Norway was going to start historically accurate to its time, where you'd first had to go down a tree about what was happening, before the 2001 election that would decide the fate of the country

Norway was going to have the ability to pick between all the modern political parties, some abstract parties, the Communists and even a made up Fascist party. Norway had a few paths to take outside of the parties, between building the country, strenghting NATO or going its own path, such as rebuilding the Kalmar Union under Norwegian rule. Norway could even fall into civil war, where all the counties would be fighting against you in the Oslo commune

However the content was never completed as i quit the team a few days after getting a heart surgery, and due to some other factors like team tension and lack of progress and team motivaton. The content may never see the light of day as the original team was motivated behind a political reason, and wished to focus more on the story rather than the gameplay.

"THE RUSH GAMEMODE"

2025 - 2026

Being a vivid enjoyer of the Battlefield games, i came across an idea somethime during the creation of Gullypass about that that i could try to make a gamemode where it would play exactly like it would be with Battlefield's Rush Gamemode

Rush is a gamemode from the Battlefield: Bad Company where one team attacks and the other defends a series of objectives. Attackers must plant and detonate explosives on designated targets, while defenders try to stop them by disarming charges or eliminating the opposing team. As objectives are destroyed, the battle progresses across the map, creating a focused, front-line experience.

The Rush that i made for TF2 works exactly the same way, except that since there is no interact button, you must stand near the objective to arm or disarm the objective

Starting early on as i tested how the objectives could work, this was around the time i started using AI to reduce workflow and increase output of what i could do as such within a week i had perfected a type of system that would be playable, and play like Rush where you'd need to arm and defuse the objective

During the construction of the gamemode with the help of my Artist, i personally reached out to voice actors to voice the gamemode, concept artists that could envision the characther i had made for the announcer, modellers who could create the assets needed and later on particle makers to make particles for the gamemode.
And due to that, i was able to reach most of the goals i had in mind for creating Rush for TF2

Having made the gamemode, i now had to present it in a way where it could gain interest, and so within a bit of a time i made a map for it

"FLOWERPOT"

2025

Flowerpot was developed in just under two months but we're delayed slightly by two weeks since i had to take surgery at the time, which rendered me unable to finish the map with the gamemode in it. It is quite a small map, designed in such a way that each pathway would lead to these objectives that are quite placed linear. Destroying one over the other objective would always lead to an advantage in a different way

Set around a dust valley, with what is supposed to be the behind the Valve map "Granary", it follows as the characther "Wendel" plots both of the teams to fight over a piece of land, to destroy Computer stations that are layed across the map, that sends power signals to the objectives that are in the regular maps. Destroying them would render them inoperable, which is why the characther tasked the other team to defend them

Rush & Flowerpot released to relativly positve reviews about its new type of gameplay, its grapichal style, but was critized due to its simiratity between Control Points due its similar gameplay and HUD Layout

While not having a sense of control points, as you destroy the objectives by planting bombs on them, and the other way around by defusing them, players quickly took quick descisions on the gamemode by primarly looking at the difference between the HUD and the one i made for Rush

The gamemode was primarly made so that mappers/devs could experiment on creating maps that would contain destructible environments

However due to time constraints and pressure to release the map, i had to scrap the idea of having a destructible enviroment for Source maps, and would only hope so anyone could follow up on that original idea

After the creation of the map, i would be reached out by one of the leaders of a project aiming to build a theme around maps/cosmetics/art and more, that asked if they could include the gamemode, and have me create a map and establish a team that could make more said maps for the project in the Rush mode. I gladly accepted that request

That would lead me to create the second Rush map, that you can read about in the Low Profile Collection Tab under MODS/PROJECTS

"HOLT"

2026

Somethime after the second Rush map "Monoceros" was announnced in early 2026, i was reached out by another project leader for another project called "The Northwood Project" that would end later that same year. They gave me an unlimited amount of time for the map, but i felt that i needed to improve the gamemode a bit more, so i started working on it right away

After some delays and some redesigning, Holt was released after 3 months in development, sporting a style that would match the projects goal

Holt is set in alpine enviroment somewhere in Europe between some mountains, in a an important fuel depot, where the story simiarly unfolds like Flowerpot, with Wendel tasking the teams to destroy the Computer Stations

Holt released to relativly positve reviews about its new type of gameplay and its grapichal style, but was critized due to its Scale compared to other maps. At this point people had warmed up more to the gamemode, which helped the maps reputation a bit better, as people wasn't complaining so much about the gamemode anymore

Being the last Rush map, it was also the last Source map i would produce before i quit the Source Engine to persue a more intesive game development role

"POST RELEASE"

2026

Post release of the gamemode i contiuned to develop on the gamemode vigerously by doing a lot of playtesting, and taking a lot of feedback from various amounts of sources. A lot of the issues of the gamemode, such as losse voice lines, visual indication and gameplay phasing we're vital to fix in order to get the message of the gamemode done correctcly. It was only until Holt in 2026 where i perfected the gamemode

As the gamemode grew i would contiune non development as a mentor, helping any future mappers that took an interest in the map, by solving bugs that they could't solve themself

Check the Low Profile Collection Tab under MODS/PROJECTS to see what maps i mentored prior to quiting Source

"THE LOW PROFILE COLLECTION"

2025 - 2026

The Low Profile Collection was a secrative community update for "Team Fortress 2" centered around the Spytech theme. It released with a trailer, 10 maps, countless of cosmetics, paints and the Rush Gamemode as a collaboration inclusion.

I was recruited to the project somethime after i made the map Flowerpot to showcase the Rush Gamemode, and we're told to keep the project in absolute secrecy up to the announcement of the project

My role for the project was a Coder, Mapper and Gamemode Manager for Rush and other gamemodes that might be made. With that i set togheter a temporary discord to organize a team of developers who would be developing 4 Rush maps for the project

Over the span of a year i would work togheter with more than 60 people from things like the projects promotional material, the projects trailer, and by helping recruit more developers to the development of the project

As per requested they wished me to create a map for the project, shortly after joining i begun the map i would create development

"MONOCEROS"

2025 - 2026

Monoceros is the second Rush map made by me the creator of the gamemode, and the first Rush map for the Low Profile Project.
It is a map set in a canyon somewhere in America about where the BLU team infiltrates a massive facility to blow up its infastrcture, then for them to move onto the back of the facility where a backyard town lies

The map had a rought start where it did not play out correctly, the first version was initially made to try to make it so the areas around the objectives would be destructible, which on the top of me having to design the map around a Spytech style, made it difficult to adjust for paths and flank routes

After accounting for the feedback which we're too negative to ignore on multiple servers, i completely redesigned the map and made it into what the map would be known for today

Monoceros released in the Summer of 2025 as with the rest of the Project maps to positive receptions, from both the players and the playtesters about its gameplay and its visuals

That said, i wasn't only asked to create a singular map, as the creator of the Rush gamemode i was the Project mananger who guided people to make more Rush maps for the gamemode and the projects goal. During the duration of the project, i would be leading a team of 21 developers, whom 5 of them we're mapmakers who would be making the maps for the gamemode and the project.

My role in this was mostly to just organize people around where i hosted playtests and mentored those who we're making the map's, such as rooting out bugs or on how they should design their maps

"MENTOR - FULIGO"

"VIPRACE GAMEMODE"

2026

Somethime after i developed Monoceros, i was frantically messaged by one of the directing writers of the mode "Mobster VIP" for TF2, who asked me to step in for a role as coder as the leading coder for the said mode, could not step in to make the request

VIP is a gamemode about escorting a characther that usually is pretty defenceless, across a map to the very end of it, where upon reaching the end of it the round is won for those who we're escorting the VIP, while the other team who we're supposed to kill the VIP will lose.
VIP was scrapped for Team Fortress 2 during its development between 1996 and 2007 where it eventually became the gamemode Payload.
When Vscript was introduced to TF2 in 2022, VIP was recreated for TF2 reimagined in a new way that would make it fun dubbed "Mobster VIP", hence the characther who is a Mobster. They gave the VIP a weapon and had him be more a supportive/attacking role

I was asked to recreate VIPR, which is a new spin on the VIP gamemode that was introduced by the standalone game "Team Fortress 2: Classified", in which there we're two VIP's instead of 1, that would fight to combat over territory, instead of being escorted.

Having learned the full potential of using AI to code backend code for you, i spent a full two weeks coding the gamemode in ChatGPT then later Claude and rubberducking the code to see if it would work, creating over 5 varients of it based upon the requests i was getting from the said writer, and then ending up with a version that would work on the said 2 week mark

Something that would otherwise have taken 5 months, was done in under 2 weeks. Having completed the code, i contiuned to refine it, and made temporary models that could be swapped out for new custom ones, setting up the code nessecary for the modellers to take over, who would take the most time to finish the gamemode

To test VIPR, we used the Valve maps Powerhouse and Viaduct, which was requested by those from the VIP Mobster team, that i edited to have the VIPR gamemode in it

Over the course of a few months we would contiune to refine the gamemode and the abilities of the characthers, until the models and maps nessecary was done for the modes original vision to be completed

VIPR was received possitivly, with most people saying it was quite a fun spin for a reimagination of the gamemode

"GRENADE MODE"

2026

During the development of VIPR, i was contacted by the orginal pitcher and directing modeller for the playermodels of the VIP mobster gamemode, with a proposal for a another gamemode. But as they explained to me it wasn't exactly going to be a gamemode and more like a map gimmick

This was Grenade Mode, and grenade mode attempted to bring back the cut grenades from the TF2 Beta in 2006, by means that we're possible within Vscript.
Unlike the VIPR gamemode, Grenade mode was not a gamemode, but a map gimmick, meaning that it could be placed around like the halloween spellbooks on Valve's Halloween maps, enabling players to use grenades over spellbooks

The mode initially worked by tick detecting whenever a player had a Halloween Spellbook, forcing them to have a specific spell, then giving them a grenade as per function, then detecing when they threw it, threw a prop grenade instead that would explode after a certain amount of seconds.
It had 9 class specific grenades based on the selection given in the previous installment Team Fortess Classic, and 5 custom made grenades that i came up with, if the mode was set to be Random

Grenade mode had a rough start, with it being plagued by unexplained server lag that i tried to combat several times, this came about from a combination of how many functionalities we're being run at the same time, and any potential leak that would come about from the code that was being drawn from

The mode was initially coded in ChatGPT, but was later coded in Claude with supervision from me, a much more superiour coding AI that would help out root the initial problems of the mode

To test the mode, we used the Valve map Dustbowl who we're originally going to have Grenades, hence hints by some of the locations who still leaves around marks from the Beta. Grenade placements we're initially based upon those from Team Fortress Classic, but what later on added more of as the mode was tested

Grenade Mode was received possitivly, with most people saying it was pretty interesting with the grenades returning, and how TF2 might have turned out with them

And since all Paradox games shares the same bracket language, this would be quite an easy job

The difficult part of creating the mod would not to find the backgrounds in Stellaris localization, it would be to apply the flag overlay.
i would first try to make a transparent background that could be applied to any background on a flag, but that did not work, so in the end i ended up finding a white flag, scaling that up, then placing that in a layer set to be "Hard Light". This essentially makes it so the layer/image is lighted on the layer below it per its color.
I would sit for a bit on the mod to try perfect it, going from different types of overlays, like curtains to see what could make the best display for a flag.

And when i had finished a background layer for the flag, instead of having to manually apply that for every single 50 or so flags that we're in Stellaris, i had claude take the flag overlay, then apply that for me for all the flags under 10 seconds, saving me a few hours of me doing it instead. And as such i had made the "Flag Overlay" mod for Stellaris
